# Yukihiro Matsumoto (Matz): Creator of Ruby

## Background

**Full Name**: Yukihiro Matsumoto
**Nickname**: Matz
**Born**: April 14, 1965
**Nationality**: Japanese
**Famous For**:
- Creator of the Ruby programming language
- "MINASWAN" cultural influence
- Programmer happiness philosophy
- Chief Architect of Ruby

## Major Contributions

### Ruby Programming Language (1993-1995)

Matz began work on Ruby in 1993 and released it publicly in 1995:
- Object-oriented scripting language
- Inspired by Perl, Smalltalk, Eiffel, Ada, and Lisp
- Focus on programmer happiness
- "Principle of Least Surprise"

**Motivation**: "I wanted a language that made me productive while being fun to use."

**Goal**: More powerful than Perl, more object-oriented than Python.

**Sources**:
- [Wikipedia - Yukihiro Matsumoto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yukihiro_Matsumoto)
- [Artima - The Philosophy of Ruby](https://www.artima.com/articles/the-philosophy-of-ruby)

### Ruby on Rails Enablement

While not created by Matz, Ruby's design enabled David Heinemeier Hansson's Rails framework (2004):
- Revolutionized web development
- "Convention over configuration"
- Rapid application development
- Made Ruby mainstream

**Impact**: Ruby became one of the top programming languages for web development.

## Programming Philosophy

### Programmer Happiness

**Core philosophy**: "Ruby is designed to make programmers happy."

**Principle**: Optimize for programmer joy, not just machine efficiency.

**Quote**: "I want to make Ruby users free and give them the freedom to choose."

**Implementation**:
- Expressive syntax
- Multiple ways to accomplish tasks
- Natural, not simple
- Fun to use

**Sources**:
- [Evrone - Yukihiro Matsumoto Interview](https://evrone.com/blog/yukihiro-matsumoto-interview)
- [Two-Bit History - The Ruby Story](https://twobithistory.org/2017/11/19/the-ruby-story.html)

### Natural Over Simple

**Quote**: "I am trying to make Ruby natural, not simple, in a way that mirrors life."

**Philosophy**: Language should feel natural to humans, even if that means not being minimalistic.

**Reasoning**: "Language is kind of a scaffolding of the mind, a way of structuring your thinking."

**Contrast**: Unlike minimalist languages that restrict options, Ruby provides expressiveness.

### Multiple Ways (TMTOWTDI)

**Principle**: Inherited from Perlâ€”"There's More Than One Way To Do It"

**Matz's take**: "I would rather provide many ways if it's possible, but encourage or guide users to choose a better way."

**Balance**:
- Flexibility to express ideas naturally
- Guidance toward better practices
- Room for programmer creativity
- Not forcing one "right" way

**Source**: [Artima - The Philosophy of Ruby](https://www.artima.com/articles/the-philosophy-of-ruby)

### Principle of Least Surprise

**Common attribution**: Ruby follows "Principle of Least Surprise"

**Matz's clarification**: "The principle of least surprise means principle of least my surprise. And it means the principle of least surprise after you learn Ruby very well."

**Reality**: "Actually, I didn't make the claim that Ruby follows the principle of least surprise. Someone felt the design of Ruby follows that philosophy, so they started saying that."

**Real principle**: Ruby should feel natural and intuitive once you understand it, not necessarily on first encounter.

**Source**: [Yukihiro Matsumoto - Wikiquote](https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Yukihiro_Matsumoto)

## Community Culture: MINASWAN

### "Matz Is Nice And So We Are Nice"

**Origin**: Matz's demeanor inspired the Ruby community motto "Matz is nice and so we are nice," commonly abbreviated as MINASWAN.

**Impact**:
- Welcoming community culture
- Helpful, friendly interactions
- Low tolerance for rudeness
- Mentorship and teaching valued

**Philosophy**: Technical community doesn't have to be hostile; kindness and technical excellence coexist.

**Sources**:
- [The Ruby Story](https://twobithistory.org/2017/11/19/the-ruby-story.html)

## Design Influences

### Larry Wall (Perl)

**Influence**: "Larry Wall is my hero"

**Adopted**: Multiple ways to do things, text processing power, practical philosophy

**Difference**: Ruby more consistently object-oriented than Perl

### Object-Oriented Purity

**Goal**: Everything is an object, more consistently than Python

**Philosophy**: Pure object model is more elegant and easier to reason about

**Implementation**: Even primitives are objects in Ruby

## Technical Philosophy

### Blocks and Closures

**Innovation**: Ruby's blocks made closures accessible and natural

**Philosophy**: Functional programming concepts should be easy and natural, not academic

**Impact**: Influenced how blocks/lambdas are designed in other languages

### Metaprogramming

**Philosophy**: Language should allow elegant metaprogramming

**Features**:
- Open classes
- method_missing
- define_method
- Elegant DSL creation

**Impact**: Enabled Rails and other frameworks to create clean, expressive APIs

### Duck Typing

**Philosophy**: "If it walks like a duck and quacks like a duck, it's a duck"

**Principle**: Care about behavior, not class hierarchy

**Benefit**: Flexibility and expressiveness over rigid type systems

- Expressive languages enable faster development

## Lessons from Yukihiro Matsumoto

### For Language Designers

1. **Optimize for humans**: Programmer experience matters more than theoretical purity
2. **Allow flexibility**: Multiple ways enable natural expression
3. **Consider fun**: Enjoyable languages attract users
4. **Community matters**: Culture shapes adoption and success
5. **Evolve thoughtfully**: Balance tradition with modernization

### For Developers

1. **Happiness matters**: Choose tools that make you productive and happy
2. **Be nice**: MINASWAN applies beyond Ruby
3. **Express naturally**: Use language features that feel right
4. **Learn deeply**: Principle of least surprise applies after mastery
5. **Embrace multiple ways**: Different approaches suit different problems

### For Community Leaders

1. **Lead by example**: Matz's kindness shaped Ruby culture
2. **Welcome newcomers**: Friendly communities grow faster
3. **Value mentorship**: Help others learn and grow
4. **Respect differences**: Multiple approaches have value
5. **Stay humble**: Success doesn't require arrogance
